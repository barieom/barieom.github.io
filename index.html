Barry "Barium" Eom

엄벼리 | 嚴 ビョリ

My last name is pronounced "uhm," so people call me "Barium." Disclaimer: I am not a chemistry major.
I am a student at Tufts University with curiosity to learn and strong passion in making a difference in the world. Whether it be by directly helping someone in need or tackling grievous issues like homelessness in my community, I have always cherished the fact that anyone could be of positive influence, even if small, to the world.
Majoring in computer science and cognitive and brain sciences, I am invested in the varying impact major tech companies, as well as tech startups, have on society, as they function as the main facilitator of innovation and are currently one of the biggest drivers of change in the world; in particular, I am interested in machine learning/AI and AR industry.
Currently, I am working on publishing a paper about my findings on Variance Time Course Analysis and mind wandering. Additionally, I work as a consulting project manager representing the TAMID Group for CureFacts, as well as leading weekly education seminars on varying consulting topics. I am also part of Jumbo Code, a pro-bono tech development group on campus.
From the implications of Absurdism and the meaning of life to the recent emergence of Tottenham Hotspurs and fantasy football, I enjoy talking about a lot of things. If you would like to reach out, feel free to contact me at: b.eom[at]tufts.edu. (Change [at] to @)

Other Projects and Researches

Coffeehouse for a Cause

I founded this fundraiser my sophomore year of high school. The intention was to cultivate a culture of giving in Belmont, MA, a rather affluent town isolated from homelessness. Initially planned as a minor event, Coffeehouse for a Cause gained a lot of attention from many residents of the town and is now an yearly tradition, having raised more than $15,000 to combat homelessness in the Greater Boston Area. I am working on establishing another coffeehouse based fundraiser at Tufts University next fall.

Consulting final deliverable for CureFacts

Cognitive sciences research on standardizing the measurement of mind-wandering, a crudely subjective state of mind. I executed this by writing more than a dozen of data processing scripts in MATLAB and Python to elucidate any correlation between mind wandering and reaction time, an objective form of measuring mind wandering
